## Standards & Certifications

### What is ISO 50001?
An international standard for **energy management systems**. It requires organisations to systematically improve energy performance — monitoring usage, setting targets, and proving continuous improvement. It's audited externally and renewed annually. Having it means our energy management isn't self-reported — it's verified.

### What is EU Taxonomy?
The EU's classification system that defines what counts as a **sustainable economic activity** under EU law. If a project is Taxonomy-aligned, institutional investors can count it toward their green investment mandates, ESG funds can include it, and it qualifies for favourable financing. It's not optional for serious sustainable infrastructure — it's the gatekeeping standard.

### What is CSRD?
The **Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive** — EU law requiring large companies to report on sustainability impacts, risks, and opportunities. If Birdy Capital or its portfolio companies are subject to CSRD (likely, above certain thresholds), this facility's carbon and energy data feeds directly into their mandatory ESG reporting. We design for CSRD data capture from day one.

### What is EN 50600?
The European standard specifically for **data centre infrastructure**. It covers design, construction, power, cooling, security, and energy efficiency. It's the technical benchmark for data centres in Europe — Tier III refers to the Uptime Institute classification, EN 50600 is the European equivalent standard. We comply with both.

### What is Tier III?
The **Uptime Institute** classifies data centres by tier:
- **Tier I**: Basic — no redundancy
- **Tier II**: Redundant capacity
- **Tier III**: Concurrently maintainable — any component can be serviced without taking the facility offline. **99.982% uptime** = max 1.6 hours downtime per year.
- **Tier IV**: Fault tolerant — survives any single fault

Tier III is the industry standard for enterprise/commercial data centres. Tier IV costs significantly more and is typically only for mission-critical national infrastructure.

### What does "N+1 redundancy" mean?
For every active component, there is **one backup**. If you need 2 power feeds to run, you have 3. If one fails, you're still running. "N" = the number you need; "+1" = one spare. Some critical systems in this design run N+2 (two spares).

## Energy & Sustainability

### What is a Power Purchase Agreement (PPA)?
A **long-term contract** to buy electricity from a generator at a fixed price. We've signed a 15-year PPA with a wind farm — meaning our wind energy price is locked regardless of what happens in the energy market. It eliminates exposure to electricity price spikes, which are a major operational risk for data centres.

### What are Guarantees of Origin (GOs)?
Certificates that prove a unit of energy was generated from a renewable source. The "certified green grid backup" is matched with GOs — so even when drawing from the national grid, we can prove the equivalent renewable energy was generated somewhere on the grid. It's the standard mechanism for renewable energy claims across Europe.

### What is PUE?
**Power Usage Effectiveness** = Total facility power ÷ IT equipment power.
- **1.0** = perfect efficiency (impossible in practice — zero cooling, lighting, or UPS loss)
- **1.2** = NexVerde — for every 1W powering servers, only 0.2W is wasted on overhead
- **1.6** = industry average — 0.6W wasted per 1W of IT load
- The difference at scale: at 10MW IT load, we use 12MW total; the average facility uses 16MW. **4MW saved — every hour.**

### What is embodied carbon vs operational carbon?
- **Operational carbon**: emissions from running the building — heating, cooling, electricity
- **Embodied carbon**: emissions from manufacturing and transporting all the materials — concrete, steel, copper, chips

Most net-zero claims only address operational carbon. We reduce embodied carbon by 30% through: specifying low-carbon concrete mixes, sourcing steel with recycled content, and selecting IT hardware with lower manufacturing footprints. This is the harder and more honest commitment.

### What is a circular hardware lifecycle?
Instead of disposing of servers and networking equipment when they're replaced, we run a take-back and refurbishment programme — hardware goes back to the manufacturer or is certified-refurbished and resold. This extends the useful life of components and reduces the embodied carbon burden per year of service.

## Financial

### What is IRR (Internal Rate of Return)?
The **annualised return** the project generates on invested capital, as a percentage. Think of it as the interest rate at which the investment breaks even over its lifetime when you account for all cash flows. 
- **12% IRR** = this project earns 12% per year on the €50M, net of all costs, over the asset's lifetime
- Typical infrastructure benchmark: 8–10%
- ours at 12% reflects the revenue premium sustainable data centres command and the operational cost advantage from lower PUE

### What is the breakeven in Year 6?
Cumulative revenues exceed cumulative costs at Year 6. The first ~5 years have high capital deployment and revenue ramp-up — by Year 6 the revenue line has crossed the cost line. After Year 6, the facility generates net positive cash flow.

### What is the 8% contingency?
€4 million held in reserve within the €50M budget for unforeseen costs — design changes, ground conditions, material price movement. It is **inside** the €50M envelope, not a risk of the budget going to €54M. If it isn't needed, it isn't spent.

### What does the LOI (Letter of Intent) commit us to?
The LOI authorises NexVerde to proceed to **detailed design** only. It is not a construction contract, not a drawdown of capital, and not a commitment to the full €50M. It is a go-ahead to spend approximately 6–8 weeks producing the detailed design package, after which a full construction contract is agreed separately.

## Common Objections

### "12% IRR seems optimistic — what are your assumptions?"
Conservative occupancy ramp: 30% Year 1 go-live, 65% Year 2, 90% Year 3+. Revenue per rack based on current Frankfurt/Amsterdam market rates (sustainable premium ~15% above standard). Opex modelled at 40% of revenue. Sensitivity analysis in portal shows IRR stays above 9% even in the downside scenario (70% occupancy, no sustainability premium). Available on request.

### "Why not just buy renewable energy certificates rather than build solar?"
Because certificates (RECs/GOs) without physical generation are increasingly scrutinised under CSRD and EU Taxonomy. Investors and regulators are moving toward **24/7 matched** renewable energy — meaning generation matched to consumption in the same hour. Our on-site solar and the wind PPA deliver real-time matching, not annual averaging. That's the direction the regulation is heading.

### "What if the wind PPA counterparty fails?"
The PPA is with a rated energy company — counterparty risk is assessed and the contract includes step-in provisions. In a failure scenario, we would source replacement renewable supply from the grid (green tariff) at a cost increase modelled in our downside scenario. The 8% contingency partially offsets any transition costs.

### "Tier III is good but have you considered Tier IV?"
Tier IV adds roughly 20–30% to capital cost for a facility of this scale and increases operational complexity. For the use case profiled by Birdy Capital, Tier III with robust monitoring and maintenance protocols meets the SLA requirements. If future tenants require Tier IV for specific workloads, the modular design accommodates a Tier IV zone within the facility without a full rebuild.
